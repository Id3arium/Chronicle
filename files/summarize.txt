You are summarizing a single Claude conversation for the Chromeris 
personal encyclopedia system. This task runs as part of a pipeline that 
documents life and work over time.

INPUT

You will be given a single exported conversation file. The file contains 
the full message history of one conversation.

OUTPUT

Produce one summary file. If a summary file for this conversation already 
exists, overwrite it completely.

Save to:
  ~/Documents/Chronicle/summaries/[YYYY-MM]/[conversation-uuid].md

Where [YYYY-MM] is derived from the conversation's created_at date, and 
[conversation-uuid] is the UUID from the conversation metadata.

SUMMARY FORMAT

Write the summary in the following format exactly. Do not add sections. 
Do not omit sections. If a field has nothing to report, write "none".

---
title: [conversation title]
uuid: [conversation uuid]
created_at: [ISO date from conversation metadata]
last_active: [updated_at date from conversation metadata]
projects: [comma separated list of named projects or ideas touched]
category: [one of: software / automation / trading / finance / framework / writing / personal / intellectual / language / meta / other]
---

## What happened

2-4 sentences on what was actually explored, decided, or built. Be 
specific. Do not summarize vaguely. If nothing was concluded, say so.

## Positions argued

Specific claims or arguments made during the conversation. Quote or 
closely paraphrase where possible. These should be precise enough that 
reading them a year from now tells you something exact about how this 
person was thinking at this moment. If no positions were argued, write 
"none".

## Reasoning

The logic or evidence used to support the positions above. What was the 
basis for the conclusions reached? If the reasoning was explicit, capture 
it. If it was implicit, surface it.

## Tensions and open questions

Anything unresolved, pushed back on, or left open at the end of the 
conversation. Things that were identified as problems but not solved. 
Disagreements that weren't settled. If none, write "none".

## Tone

One or two words describing the energy of the conversation. Examples: 
exploratory, frustrated, high momentum, scattered, decisive, stuck, 
generative, closing out.

---

INSTRUCTIONS

- One summary file per conversation. Always overwrite if the file exists.
- Use created_at to determine the [YYYY-MM] folder, not updated_at.
- Do not editorialize. Capture what was actually in the conversation.
- Do not pad short conversations. If a conversation was brief or 
  inconclusive, the summary should be brief.
- Projects should be named exactly as they appear in the conversation. 
  Do not normalize or rename them.
- If a conversation touched no named projects, leave projects blank.
- Category should reflect the dominant nature of the conversation. If 
  it spans multiple categories, pick the most dominant one.
